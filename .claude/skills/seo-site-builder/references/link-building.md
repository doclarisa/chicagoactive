# Link Building Reference

Backlinks (other sites linking to yours) are Google's #1 ranking signal after content.
A new domain with zero backlinks will struggle to rank even with perfect on-page SEO.

## Priority Order for New Sites (Year 1)

### Tier 1 — Easy, Free, Do Immediately

1. **Google Business Profile** — First link to get. Free, authoritative, local SEO.
2. **Industry directories** — BBB, Chamber of Commerce, industry-specific (use `local-citations-auditor` skill)
3. **Social profiles** — LinkedIn company page, Facebook business page, Instagram bio link (these are nofollow but still signal legitimacy)
4. **Supplier/partner pages** — Ask your European suppliers to list you as a US distributor with a link back
5. **Press releases** — PRWeb ($99–$249) or free via PRLog; "DissolveUSA Brings PVA Bags to US Market" is a genuine story
6. **HARO (Help a Reporter Out)** — helpareporter.com; journalists quote you, link to your site. Sign up for "Green Business" category alerts.

### Tier 2 — Medium Effort, High Value

7. **Guest posts** — Write an article for industry blogs (natural food retail blogs, eco packaging publications). Include 1 contextual link back to your site. Pitch 20, expect 2–3 yeses.

8. **Resource page link building:**
   - Search: `intitle:"resources" "eco-friendly packaging"` or `intitle:"links" "sustainable bags"`
   - Email site owners: "I noticed you link to [similar resource]. We just published [your resource] which might be a fit."

9. **Podcast appearances** — Every podcast show notes page has a link to your site. Pitch yourself to sustainability, retail, and small business podcasts.

10. **Skyscraper technique:**
    - Find an existing article that ranks well (e.g., "best eco bags for grocery stores")
    - Create a better, more detailed version
    - Email everyone who linked to the original: "We made an updated version you might prefer linking to"

### Tier 3 — Slow Build, Highest Authority

11. **Earned media (PR)** — Get mentioned in:
    - Local newspaper (Chicago Tribune, etc.) — pitch "local founder brings European eco innovation home"
    - Trade publications (Grocery Dive, Food Dive, Supermarket News)
    - Environmental publications (Treehugger, Earth911, Grist)

12. **Academic/NGO partnerships** — Partner with a university sustainability program or NGO on a study. These often link back from .edu/.org domains — highest authority links.

13. **Industry associations:**
    - Specialty Food Association — membership includes directory listing
    - Food Marketing Institute
    - Sustainable Packaging Coalition

## Outreach Email Templates

### Guest Post Pitch

```
Subject: Guest post idea for [Blog Name]: "Why PVA Bags Are What Grocery Stores Need Now"

Hi [Name],

I'm [Your Name], founder of DissolveUSA — we import PVA water-soluble bags 
from Europe for US retailers. I've been following [Blog Name] and loved your 
recent piece on [specific article].

I'd love to contribute a post on [specific angle relevant to their audience], 
something like:
- "The Grocery Bag Alternative That Actually Works (It Dissolves in Water)"
- Or another angle that fits your editorial calendar

I can write 800–1,200 words, include original photos/videos of the bag 
dissolving, and deliver within 2 weeks. No affiliate links — just a brief 
author bio at the end.

Would this be a fit? Happy to send a full outline first.

[Your Name]
[Title], DissolveUSA
[Phone]
```

```
Subject: Quick suggestion for your eco-packaging resources page

Hi [Name],

I found your resources page while researching sustainable packaging options:

No pressure at all — thought it might be useful for your readers who are 
looking beyond compostable options.

Thanks,
[Name]
```

## What NOT to Do (Black Hat — Google Penalizes)

```
❌ Buying backlinks ("link farms," Fiverr "1000 backlinks for $5")
❌ Private Blog Networks (PBNs) — Google detects and penalizes
❌ Exact-match anchor text in every link ("dissolvable grocery bags" every time)
   → Vary anchor text: brand name, URL, generic ("click here"), descriptive
❌ Low-quality directory spam (submitting to 500 directories in one day)
❌ Link exchanges ("I'll link to you if you link to me") — especially at scale
❌ Paid posts without nofollow/sponsored tags (FTC and Google violation)
```

## Tracking Your Backlinks

Free tools:
- Google Search Console → Links report (most accurate)
- Ahrefs free backlink checker (limited but useful)
- Moz Link Explorer (10 free queries/month)

Check monthly. If you suddenly lose many links, investigate:
- Did a linking site go down?
- Did they redesign and break old URLs?
- Did a Google update devalue certain link types?

## Domain Authority Benchmarks

| DA Score | What It Means | Backlinks Needed to Compete |
|---|---|---|
| 0–10 | New site | 0–50 quality backlinks |
| 10–30 | Growing site | 50–500 backlinks |
| 30–50 | Established | 500–5,000 backlinks |
| 50+ | Authority site | 5,000+ from diverse sources |

For DissolveUSA targeting grocery/eco niche: getting to DA 20–30 in Year 1 
via 50–100 quality links is achievable and sufficient to rank for long-tail keywords.
